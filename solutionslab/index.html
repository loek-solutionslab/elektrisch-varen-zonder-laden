chapter 01

technical
the easy wins.

These two issues are fixable in an afternoon on the current Wix site — no rebuild needed.

No H1 heading on any page
HIGH

Every page of the site uses H2 tags as its main heading — no H1 anywhere. This is one of the strongest signals Google uses to understand what a page is about. Without it, search engines have to guess your topic from surrounding content. Fix: set the top heading on each page to H1.

Page title is 76 characters
LOW

Current title: "Elektrisch varen op zonne-energie. Zorgeloos en zonder opladen het water op." Google truncates at ~60 characters in results, so your tail is getting cut. Shortening to something like "Elektrisch varen op zonne-energie | Elektrisch Varen Zonder Laden" (61 chars) shows in full and includes the brand.

chapter 02

marketing
the big leaks.

Here's what stops visitors from becoming buyers. For each leak we show what the site says today and what it should say instead. The "better" version isn't invented — it comes from your own content, rewritten for the visitor.

01

The headline speaks engineer, not customer.

NOW
"Elektrisch varen op zonne-energie. Zorgeloos en zonder opladen het water op."
Long, technical, feature-focused. It describes what the boat *does* instead of what it gives the visitor.
BETTER
Vaar onbeperkt.
Vaar stil.
Vaar op de zon.
Three words, an emotional benefit each. Matches the 3-second attention span of a cold visitor and immediately communicates value.
02

The main CTA is "look in the menu above".

NOW
"Kijk in het menu boven om te zien welke verschillende mogelijkheden er zijn, of vraag naar informatie via de contactpagina."
This is the worst CTA a product site can have — it puts the work on the visitor and offers zero urgency or specificity.
BETTER
Plan uw vrijblijvende proefvaart →
Bel +31 622 199 699
Ontdek welke boot bij u past →
Three CTAs ordered by commitment. Each one concrete, verb-first, removes friction.
03

Nine out of ten prices say "op aanvraag".

NOW
Only the Commodore (€32.500) and Solara (€9.900) show prices. The other 8 boats say "Op aanvraag".
Hiding prices is common in this category — but it forces visitors into an email exchange before they know if you're in their budget. Many simply leave.
BETTER
Show "vanaf"-prices for all 10 boats.
Even a starting price anchors the visitor's expectations. Options and configuration conversations happen after — not before — qualifying interest.
04

Zero social proof — nowhere.

NOW
No customer testimonials, no reviews, no "aantal tevreden klanten", no certifications, no media mentions, no expert endorsements.
A visitor considering a €30k+ purchase has no proof anyone else has trusted you with theirs.
BETTER
Add 3 customer stories with photos.
Real owners, real water, real first names. Add a number ("X sloepen op Nederlands water") and any certifications you hold. This alone moves serious buyers off the fence.
05

The page has no customer journey.

NOW
Flow: long header paragraph → product grid → FAQ → footer. Visitors get specifications before they understand the benefit. No trust-building moments. No guided "which boat fits me".
BETTER
Headline → Benefits → Social proof → Fleet → How it works → FAQ → CTA
A visitor moves from curiosity to trust to action. Each section answers the next question before it's asked. We rebuilt the demo this way — compare side by side.
